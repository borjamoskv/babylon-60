Evidence standard

Claims should point to artifacts, not confidence theater.

Material on this domain should make it clear what is proven, what is inferred, what is pending, and what cannot be published safely.

No unverifiable certification claims

Security and compliance language must name the evidence available and the assurance still missing.

No secret-bearing artifacts

Prompts, tenant payloads, keys, tokens, and private incident data do not belong in public evidence.

Facts before interpretation

Research material should keep source observations, inference, confidence, and gaps visibly separate.

Reproducible where possible

Evidence claims should point to a command, test, document, commit, or reviewable artifact.

Boundary

Trust page, not a certification badge.

CORTEX can publish evidence, posture, test results, disclosure routes, and reviewable architecture. That does not by itself create SOC 2, ISO, legal compliance, tax advice, or regulator approval. Those claims need separate audits, current legal review, and explicit scope.
