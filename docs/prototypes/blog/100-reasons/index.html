100 Reasons CORTEX Makes Better Cuts Than Anyone

Mar 5, 2026 · By borjamoskv.com · 12 min read

Every AI context window ends. Claude's 200K tokens, GPT-4o's 128K, Gemini's 1M — they all run out eventually. And when they do, someone decides what to cut. What gets preserved, what gets dropped, and how confidently you can recover it — that's the real battleground of AI memory.

Most tools treat context overflow as a data disposal problem. CORTEX treats it as a trust problem. Here are 100 reasons why our approach wins.

"The question isn't what fits in the context window. The question is what survives outside it — and whether you can prove it's intact."

🔬 Precision: What Gets Cut and Why

  • #01Entropy scoring per fact. Every stored fact has an entropy weight. Low-signal facts get cut first, not just old ones.
  • #02Type-aware eviction. Decisions are sacred. Errors are ephemeral. CORTEX never evicts a decision to preserve a log.
  • #03Graph topology matters. A fact with 12 edge connections is never cut before a fact with 0. Relationships determine survival.
  • #04TTL is semantic, not chronological. A year-old architectural decision lives longer than a 5-minute failed experiment.
  • #05Ghost promotion. Incomplete tasks are promoted before they're cut — never silently evicted.
  • #06Cross-project bridges survive. Patterns that transferred between projects are flagged as high-value and protected from eviction.
  • #07Confidence scoring on cuts. Every eviction decision is rated C1–C5. A C1 inference is always cut before a C5 confirmed fact.
  • #08Access frequency compounds retention. Facts retrieved 40+ times in 48h trigger automatic TTL extension.
  • #09Biological circadian cycles. Consolidation mimics sleep — high-traffic facts from the day are merged and strengthened overnight.
  • #10Tombstone audit trail. Nothing is silently deleted. Every eviction writes a tombstone with timestamp, reason, and hash.

🔒 Integrity: What Survives is Provably Intact

  • #11SHA-256 hash chain. Every surviving fact is cryptographically linked to its predecessor. Tampering breaks the chain visibly.
  • #12Merkle checkpoints every 100 facts. Batch integrity verification in O(log n) — not O(n).
  • #13WBFT consensus on writes. In multi-agent environments, ⅔ supermajority required before any fact enters the ledger.
  • #14AES-256-GCM at rest. The encryption layer includes authentication — you know if a byte changed, not just if it was read.
  • #15Falsification engine active. CORTEX actively attempts to disprove stored facts before promoting them to C5.
  • #16No silent exits. Every eviction, every write, every read is logged to the immutable audit ledger.
  • #17Zero-trust ingress guard. 11 PII/secret patterns checked before any fact enters storage. Keys never persist accidentally.
  • #18Replay protection. Duplicate facts are detected via semantic hash — not just exact match.
  • #19Version lineage preserved. Every update keeps the original version in the ledger. You can reconstruct any state at any point in time.
  • #20One-command compliance export. cortex compliance-report generates EU AI Act Article 12 proof in seconds.
